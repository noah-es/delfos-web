Investment Strategy vs. S&P 500

This report summarizes a Monte Carlo backtesting analysis comparing our systematic investment strategy to the S&P 500 (SPY). The study covers 2023-01-01 to 2025-07-01, using 100 simulated backtests with 6‑month rebalancing and parameter perturbations for sensitivity checks.

Mathematical summary (concise): let R_T denote the final cumulative return of a simulated run and S_T the benchmark return. We report the sample median, mean μ, standard deviation σ, interquartile range (25th–75th), the 5th–95th percentile band, compound annual growth rate (CAGR) and Sharpe ratio. Sharpe is computed as Sharpe = (μ − R_f) / σ with risk‑free rate R_f = 0.0325.

Key parameters
  • Period: 2023‑01‑01 → 2025‑07‑01
  • Simulations: 100 Monte Carlo backtests
  • Rebalancing: every 6 months
  • Risk‑free rate: 0.0325 (annual)
  • Sensitivity: small parameter perturbations applied to clustering and optimization inputs
How to read the figures
  • Fan chart: median (blue), 25th–75th and 5th–95th percentile bands — shows dispersion of paths.
  • Return histogram: empirical distribution of final returns R_T across simulations; red line = SPY final return.
  • Robustness plot: individual simulation paths (grey) compared to SPY (red) to inspect consistency.

Interpretation checklist: look for a concentrated return distribution (low σ), median and mean above the benchmark, stable outperformance across percentiles, and a Sharpe ratio that meaningfully exceeds the benchmark's risk‑adjusted return.
